SHOTCRETING

Shotcreting is a modern and efficient process of applying concrete under pressure to various surfaces. it is used in low-rise and high-rise construction projects where additional strength, stability, and protection of structural elements are required. the process allows concrete to be applied evenly to the substrate, even in hard-to-reach areas, and ensures a compact, denser, and stronger structure compared to traditional concreting.

HYDROPHOBIC BARRIERS

hydrophobic barriers are an effective solution for protecting walls and structures from capillary moisture rise, which often causes plaster deterioration, damage to wallpaper, stains, and an unpleasant damp odor. this is a process in which a special hydrophobic material is introduced into the wall or foundation structure, preventing water from moving through capillary pathways.

INJECTING

Injecting is a process of introducing special substances, most often aqueous solutions or resins, into structural elements or soil to improve their strength, stability, and water resistance. it is used for filling cracks, sealing foundations, preventing capillary moisture rise, and reinforcing soil or concrete structures.
